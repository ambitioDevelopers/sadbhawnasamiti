Creating Imapct

Better Life for People

"Chalo Kuch karte hain"

This program has around 8000 women who are part of our Self Help Groups. They are being trained with the help of our team in pickle, jam, mungodi, buknu making activities. These products are sold in the market giving these women some money which helps them have earnings on a regular basis

"Phir Ek Koshish"

This program focuses on training the children in conflict with law in different entrepreneurial courses. It is a blend of course related skills as well as soft skills in order to let these children assume constructive roles in the society and also be independent.

"Kuch Aur Jaane"

This program focuses on creating awareness amongst the backward and minority groups on issues resonating to them which are not only essential for equity, but also constitutes a critical element in our fight for poverty reduction, economic growth and strengthening of civil society. Women and children are always the worst sufferers in a poverty stricken family and need support hence informing them the government policies and the ways to have access to different amenities at a subsidised rate.

"Shelter Home"

Our shelter home is for children with disabilities where they are provided with accommodation, education, life skills, health care and vocational training in order to mainstream them in the society by making them independent and stigma ridden.

"Health Camp"

Regular health camps are conducted especially for women, people with disabilities and elderly people in backward areas where the availability of services is scarce and the need is high.

"Koi Deewana Kehta Hai"

It is dedicated to the work and life of late Dr. K. N. Mishra (founder of Sadbhwana Samiti) who himself was a poet. This event shall commence with giving gifts (like wheelchair, crutches etc) to the specially abled children and their parents.
